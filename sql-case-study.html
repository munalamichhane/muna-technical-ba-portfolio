Project Overview

This project demonstrates how SQL-driven analysis can support business decision-making. The focus is on analyzing operational and marketing-related datasets to identify trends, define KPIs, and translate analytical findings into actionable insights for stakeholders.

Emphasis is on business questions, analytical reasoning, and communication of insights, rather than just writing queries.

Business Context

Organizations often collect large volumes of operational and campaign data but struggle to extract meaningful insights aligned with business objectives. Stakeholders need clear answers to questions such as:

  • Which campaigns or activities are performing well?
  • Where are resources being underutilized?
  • What trends indicate potential risks or opportunities?

This project simulates a real-world scenario where a Technical Business Analyst uses SQL to analyze data and support data-driven decisions.

Stakeholders

  • Business stakeholders (marketing or operations managers)
  • Data consumers (analytics or strategy teams)
  • Technical teams (data engineers or developers maintaining the database)

The Technical Business Analyst acts as a bridge between raw data and stakeholder understanding.

Objectives

  • Understand business questions and define analytical goals
  • Identify relevant KPIs and metrics
  • Use SQL to query and analyze structured datasets
  • Identify trends, patterns, and anomalies
  • Translate findings into clear business insights and recommendations

Dataset Description

The dataset represents operational or marketing activity data and includes fields such as:

  • Campaign or activity identifiers
  • Dates and timestamps
  • Performance metrics (e.g., impressions, conversions, revenue, usage counts)
  • Status or category fields

The dataset structure supports aggregation, filtering, and trend analysis using SQL.

Key Business Questions

  • Which campaigns or activities show the highest performance over time?
  • How do performance metrics vary by category or channel?
  • Are there seasonal or time-based trends affecting outcomes?
  • Which areas underperform relative to business expectations?

Analytical Approach

1. KPI Definition

Key performance indicators were defined in collaboration with assumed stakeholder goals, such as:

  • Total activity volume
  • Conversion or success rate
  • Performance trends over time
  • Comparative performance across categories

2. SQL Analysis

SQL queries were written to:

  • Filter and clean relevant records
  • Aggregate metrics using GROUP BY
  • Identify trends using time-based analysis
  • Compare performance across categories or segments

Example techniques used:

  • SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY, ORDER BY
  • Aggregate functions (COUNT, SUM, AVG)
  • Date-based filtering and grouping

Focus was on clarity and correctness, ensuring results were interpretable by non-technical stakeholders.

Key Findings (Sample)

  • Certain categories or campaigns consistently outperformed others based on defined KPIs
  • Performance varied significantly over time, indicating potential seasonal effects
  • A subset of activities contributed disproportionately to overall results, highlighting optimization opportunities

Business Insights & Recommendations

  • Reallocate resources toward high-performing segments
  • Investigate underperforming areas for potential process improvements
  • Use trend data to inform future planning and forecasting

Insights were framed in business language, avoiding unnecessary technical complexity.

Deliverables

  • SQL queries supporting KPI analysis
  • Summary of findings aligned with business questions
  • Clear, stakeholder-friendly explanations of trends and insights

Skills Demonstrated

  • Translating business questions into analytical requirements
  • Using SQL to analyze structured datasets
  • Identifying trends and performance drivers
  • Communicating insights to non-technical stakeholders
  • Supporting data-driven decision-making
